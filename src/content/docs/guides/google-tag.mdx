## Environment setup:

- We recommend that you use [Google Chrome browser](https://www.google.com/chrome/) to navigate the web.
- Admin Access to your WordPress Blog.

## What is Google Tag Manager?

Google Tag Manager is a tool that allows you to manage and deploy various tracking tags and code snippets on your website without the need for manual coding. It provides a user-friendly interface for marketers to track user interactions, conversions, and other important data.

## Create a Google Tag Manager Account & Generate your Tag

1. Go to [Google Tag Manager](https://marketingplatform.google.com/about/tag-manager/).
2. Click "Start for free" and sign in with your Google Account (or create one).

![Google Tag Manager](https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/skAZR2ho0erqzpXUcSx7aDHPKdvNanuobimX1r-acNmGplJl69OLFaOuoC_fJgqz7yr64deVZd2EodhkOLX8hYB9GCf2GmBm6dbrf98fLD1JBJfzADX9SDfJ2n0CHNvzYSxZuYOAoKpeb5TRj1sRnQ)

3. Click on "Create Account."

![Create Account](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/SUrJWWveX3U8m2kmPootFBtLKUw1jxJAobAh1_L_HO9RXHEAegt6Pvdetpaf_-0dNuvtzs0-HRvu8vE4f3FiEByCztG3yMpljGg8R7L-cw8ErtXZw1N5H8vAugIifbeqwW6q-CX2fEii43J4Wv6jew)

4. In the "Container Setup" tab, choose a name for your container (e.g., Company Name) and select your country.

5. Decide if you want to share your data with Google (click on "?" for more info).

![Container Setup](https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/yDxBLJ56qzHKj1tH9f9T_Ho8f3ujwJejdY6ypjuuym9gmg0njZuQkoQ7QnZ6IScsI5iIhlrh1xEQHF5MDx3bsYebjrku7kTdEgykppYD09FbTIDmjoTfI1rFwWMrBktKjlQWNdtJT1e1R6obbe1GKw)

6. Next, choose the target platform (e.g., WEB) and click "Create."

![Create Container](https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/OnJLz51pOX2EBVHTgnpm1jJM1IOIXA_LAbsHlTq73h_0zz1b0njbgvc7s0mvK1ZjUDIUVrHpCqc57xjMCya0TChmR4f3tG2qmYHwa3fMQDCyyGNTJTegFTSdYkQq1ge2UCDqGr--rMZ4xwsjx_0Q1A)

7. Read and accept "Google Tag Manager Terms of Service Agreement" and click "Yes."

![Terms of Service](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/W1vF43UlJFUjrY_SajN0FmWHG6uGxOlDVZ_RMyx4z4lYz5k0tHUbN-RuEe4Cg7LbmsnZaWv-fiLaSHmVrdQTLgHveknAYhOQXFLqMSXUuA6Jq62Nb4MQOq7SXmgymkydFP6itJTTmlJHqDgNYvegKQ)

8. You are now in your Google Tag Manager Dashboard. The next step is to go to the "Admin" tab section.

![Dashboard](https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/MeWvAmeLjh7CaAyj5yFy3tc84K3fQ4Wkc-pytya9NawVYxG6MWlnNyyb2g-CECgnFpTLWwiMWcLhIQi_6TCntKE6ut3aRwraHnBOhxdqVn0M-wp1TzW9cv6vl2BJA1FJ1Zq0BepnPiAxARmVRieAYA)

9. Click "Install Google Tag Manager."

![Install GTM](https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/RJtSPazRl8LWAlKinWZi_1jomv7mYMrgY3bXfgp_R6Q0nDY7M5kzkDI-33yUnqh7MS-wEQHUd824sMq_WiJX0qSpKhY7taC9hNH_DLfajoJMhdtMXIyfvi-8h2iDwJdKt

- Install [Tag Assistant (by Google)](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/tag-assistant-by-google/kejbdjndbnbjgmefkgdddjlbokphdefk?hl=en) Chrome Extension.
- After installation, click on the Tag Assistant icon in your Chrome Browser.
- Click "Enable" it.
- Now you should see all your working tags or if they encounter any problems. In the screenshot below, it's in Yellow because our Google Tag Manager container is empty. In the next SOP, we'll show you how to add Google Analytics to your GTM Container.
